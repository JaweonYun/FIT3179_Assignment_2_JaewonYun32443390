Introduction

The Organisation for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) is an intergovernmental organisation with 38 member countries that was founded to stimualte economic progress and world trade. The majority of OECD Members are high-income economies ranked as "very high" in the Human Development Index, and are regareded as developed countries. Australia is part of the OECD and comparisons will be made on key welfare and welling measurements including employment, housing, education, safety, healthcare, environment and life satisfaction. Comparisons will be made between OECD nations as it is one of the largest and most reliable source of comparable statistical, economic and social data.
